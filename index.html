A Masterpiece of Khmer Architecture

Angkor Wat, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a breathtaking temple complex in Cambodia. Originally constructed as a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Vishnu for the Khmer Empire, it was gradually transformed into a Buddhist temple towards the end of the 12th century.

Symbolism

The temple is a representation of Mount Meru, the home of the gods in Hindu mythology. Its five towers symbolize the five peaks of the mountain, and the moat represents the cosmic ocean.

Bas-Reliefs

The temple walls are covered with extensive bas-reliefs, depicting epic stories from Hindu mythology, historical events, and scenes of daily life in the Khmer Empire.

Construction

Built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II, the construction of Angkor Wat is estimated to have taken around 30 years, involving thousands of artisans and laborers.

Getting There

Angkor Wat is located just outside of Siem Reap, Cambodia. The city has an international airport with connections to major cities in Asia. From Siem Reap, you can hire a tuk-tuk, car, or bicycle to explore the temple complex.

Tickets & Hours

The Angkor Archaeological Park is open from 5:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Ticket options include one-day, three-day, and seven-day passes, which can be purchased at the official ticket center.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is during the dry season, from November to March. Sunrise is a particularly magical time to see the temple, though it can be crowded. Be sure to arrive early.

Etiquette

As a religious site, visitors are expected to dress modestly. This means covering shoulders and knees. Be respectful of the monks and the sacred nature of the site.
